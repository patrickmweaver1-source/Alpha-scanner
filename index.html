ⓘ Wheel Strategy & Scoring Logic tap to expand
The wheel — quick refresher
Phase 1 — Sell CSP: sell put with strike below current price on a stock you'd own. Collect premium. Tie up cash = 100 × strike.
If put expires worthless: keep premium, redeploy capital, repeat. ✓
If assigned: you own 100 shares at the strike. Use the Covered Call Helper tab to enter Phase 2.
Phase 2 — Sell Covered Call: sell call against your shares at a strike ≥ cost basis. Collect more premium.
If call expires worthless: keep premium and shares, repeat. ✓
If called away: sell shares at strike + premium, restart at Phase 1.
Score components (higher = better candidate)
• Annualized yield (40 pts) — premium ÷ cash-at-risk × 365/DTE. Primary metric.
• Liquidity (25 pts) — OI ≥ 500 + tight spread. You'll need to close, not hold.
• IV richness vs chain (15 pts) — higher IV in this strike vs chain median = juicier premium for the moneyness.
• Delta in band (10 pts) — bonus for being near 0.275 (center of 0.20–0.35).
• Tier weight (10 pts) — tier 1 blue chips score higher than tier 4 high-IV names. Wheel reward/risk is best on quality.
Tags you'll see
IV-RICH — IV in upper 33% of chain. Premium is fat for the strike — ideal wheel sell.
IV-THIN — IV in lower 33%. Premium underwhelming; consider waiting for IV expansion.
EARNINGS-RISK — earnings inside DTE window (only shown if hard-exclude is OFF).
HIGH-VOL — Tier 4 name (TSLA/MSTR/COIN-class). Premium huge, assignment frequent. Size accordingly.
TIER-1/2/3/4/ETF — quality tier of the underlying.
WIDE — bid-ask > 8% of mid. Closing the position will eat into yield.
